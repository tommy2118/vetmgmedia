DORA Metrics

  • Deployment Frequency:

    Measures how often a team deploys code to production. Higher deployment frequencies typically indicate streamlined processes and shorter development cycles.

  • Lead Time for Changes:

    Represents the time it takes for a code change to go from commit to deployment in production. Shorter lead times can improve responsiveness and reduce the risk of deployment failures.

  • Change Failure Rate:

    Measures the percentage of deployments that result in a failure, requiring a rollback or hotfix. A lower change failure rate indicates a more stable and reliable deployment process.

  • Mean Time to Recovery:

    Tracks the average time it takes for a team to recover from a failure, such as a production incident or deployment issue. A shorter mean time to recovery signifies a more resilient and adaptive team.
